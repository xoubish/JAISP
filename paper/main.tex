\begin{figure*}[t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig2_architecture.png}
    \caption{\textbf{Model architecture.}
    \textbf{(Top:)} Self-supervised pre-training by masked-band autoencoding. The ten
    input channels---Rubin $ugrizy$ (0.2$^{\prime\prime}$/px) and Euclid VIS\,$YJH$
    (0.1$^{\prime\prime}$/px)---pass through per-band convolutional stems and a two-stream ConvNeXt encoder (Rubin and Euclid branches) whose feature maps shrink spatially and deepen in channels until they converge on a common 0.4$^{\prime\prime}$/px grid. A transformer bottleneck (4 layers, 8 heads, 256-d; $\sim$9.1M parameters) mixes the $\sim$128$^2$ tokens, and per-stream decoders
    reconstruct the single held-out band under an information-weighted loss. \textbf{(Bottom:)} For downstream tasks the encoder is frozen and its
    0.4$^{\prime\prime}$/px latent features feed lightweight task heads---detection and
    astrometry in this work, with photometry and shape heads in preparation. Input and reconstruction thumbnails are the same matched ECDFS tile as Fig.~\ref{fig:data}.}
    \label{fig:arch}
\end{figure*}

    withheld and reconstructed from the other nine: Rubin $r$ (top), Euclid VIS

    reconstruction (right); panels are $48''$ on a side. The residual uses a

    reconstruction errors are a small fraction of the signal, concentrated only at
    the cores of the brightest point sources and in the faint extended wings of
    large galaxies. The annotated $r$ is the Pearson correlation between truth and

\begin{figure*}
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=\textwidth]{figures/fig4_probe.png}
    \caption{Probing the frozen foundation representation, and the motivation for the recipe upgrade from v7 ($0.8''$/px mixed-resolution, left) to v10
    ($0.4''$/px fine-scale, right). For each band a probe predicts per-source brightness from the frozen bottleneck features at detected source positions
    under a spatial hold-out; open markers are a linear (ridge) probe, filled markers a nonlinear (two-layer MLP) probe, and the connecting line is the nonlinear gain. Both checkpoints are evaluated with a single \emph{identical} probe protocol so the scores are directly comparable. Because the probe is a
    variational lower bound on the mutual information between the representation and each band ($I \gtrsim -\tfrac{1}{2}\log_2(1-R^2)$ bits for a Gaussian
    target), the markers read directly as recoverable information content. In v7 the deep Euclid VIS+NISP bands are only weakly and \emph{nonlinearly} encoded
    (NISP $J$: linear $R^2=0.12$, i.e.\ $\gtrsim0.09$ bits, rising to $0.58$ only with the nonlinear probe); in v10 they become strongly and \emph{linearly} decodable ($J$: $R^2=0.84$, $\gtrsim1.3$ bits). The Rubin $ugrizy$ bands remain present but largely nonlinearly encoded in both versions. The gain is one of representational geometry rather than capacity: the effective dimensionality of the bottleneck is essentially unchanged between versions (participation ratio $\approx5$, spectral-entropy dimension $\approx15$--$18$ of 256), so v10 reorganizes information of comparable dimensionality into a
    more linearly accessible form. The probe uses 6 tiles and $\sim$100--120 sources, so individual linear values with large negative excursions (e.g.\ v7
    $z$) are small-sample scatter; the systematic Euclid trend is robust.}
    \label{fig:foundation_probe}
\end{figure*}

\section{Astrometry}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Idea: use the detections + foundation to (a) measure positions in faint bands by transferring the sharp VIS localization, and (b) map the cross-survey concordance field -- not "beat the existing solution"
    \item How do Rubin and Euclid mosaics agree before any correction, given they are both GAIA aligned
    \item $\sim$40--50 mas raw offset is S/N dependent and dominated by per-source centering, not WCS distortion (+figure 8)
    \item including faint sources inflates the scatter but do they help with fitting the field (+figure 9)
    \item Head correcting for centering of faint things, and remeasuring the field (+figure 10)
    \item but the head has an intrinsic $\sim$17--18 mas faint-end ceiling; the classical joint 10-band fitter (6.7 mas at SNR 5) is the best position instrument
    \item injection truth test: real $\sim$2.5$\times$ gain at low SNR (VIS-localization transfer), bounded by the VIS floor
    \item centroid-emulation caveat: at the faint end the residual-vs-label metric measures emulation of the classical centroid, not truth -- why injection + Gaia are needed
    \item absolute validation against Gaia: frame tie $(+3.7,+1.9)$ mas, $4.4$--$4.8$ mas at $G<18.5$
    \item concordance field is a measurement (degree-scale $\sim$4--7 mas), not a correction: applying it does not improve held-out positions
    \item PINN vs HGP (agree at degree scale, sub-degree is solver noise)
    \item Ian's finding on centering dependence on a/b 
\end{itemize}

\section{OOD}
\begin{itemize}
    \item do results persist on out of distribution data (another field, EDF-S, no retraining)
    \item detection transfers nearly perfectly: completeness 89.6\% (OOD) vs 90.8\% (in-dist) -- figure 11
    \item astrometry: modest $\sim$5--15\% penalty (the scary $\sim$1.5$\times$ was a MAD-variance artifact, resolved with real variance) -- figure 12
    \item bright-star suppression reproduces (stronger, corr $-0.71$): origin is input normalization, not the field
    \item can be extended all sky
\end{itemize}
